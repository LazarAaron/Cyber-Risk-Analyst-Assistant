Cyber Risk and Compliance Knowledge Base

1. 20(3)/2022-CERT-In - Government of India, Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In)
These directions require mandatory reporting of cyber incidents within 6 hours, synchronization of ICT system clocks to NIC/NPL, maintaining logs for 180 days within India, and specific data localization norms for VPS/VPN/Cloud service providers to maintain customer details for 5 years.

2. The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act)
India's comprehensive data privacy law focusing on processing digital personal data. Key concepts: Data Principal (individual), Data Fiduciary (entity determining purpose), Data Processor. Emphasizes consent (free, specific, informed), Right to Erasure, Right to Grievance Redressal, and imposes heavy penalties (up to ₹250 crore for breaches). Prohibits tracking/targeted ads for children.

3. INTERNATIONAL STANDARD ISO/IEC 27005: Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments
Provides guidelines for information security risk management, supporting the general concepts specified in ISO/IEC 27001. It outlines context establishment, risk assessment (identification, analysis, evaluation), risk treatment, acceptance, communication, and monitoring/review.

4. The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0
A voluntary framework to help organizations manage cybersecurity risk based on existing standards. Its core functions are: Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. Tiers range from Tier 1 (Partial) to Tier 4 (Adaptive).

Part 1: Risk Management Fundamentals (1-25)
Objective: Testing the bot's core logic and mathematical understanding of risk.
1. What is the standard formula for risk? $Risk = Likelihood \times Impact$.
2. Define an "Asset" in cyber risk. Anything of value to an organization (data, hardware, reputation).
3. What is a "Threat Actor"? An individual or group that performs malicious acts.
4. Define "Vulnerability." A flaw or weakness that can be exploited by a threat.
5. What is "Inherent Risk"? Risk level before any controls or mitigations are applied.
6. What is "Residual Risk"? Risk remaining after security controls are implemented.
7. What is "Risk Appetite"? The amount of risk an organization is willing to accept.
8. Define "Risk Tolerance." The degree of variance from risk appetite that is acceptable.
9. What are the 4 ways to treat risk? Avoid, Mitigate, Transfer, or Accept.
10. What is "Risk Transfer"? Shifting risk to a third party (e.g., Cyber Insurance).
11. What is "Risk Avoidance"? Eliminating the activity that causes the risk.
12. What is "Risk Mitigation"? Implementing controls to reduce likelihood or impact.
13. What is "Risk Acceptance"? Choosing to live with the risk without further action.
14. What is a "Qualitative" assessment? Using scales like Low, Medium, High to rank risk.
15. What is a "Quantitative" assessment? Using monetary values (e.g., Annual Loss Expectancy).
16. Define "Likelihood." The probability that a threat will exploit a vulnerability.
17. Define "Impact." The severity of consequences if a threat occurs.
18. What is SLE? Single Loss Expectancy (Asset Value × Exposure Factor).
19. What is ARO? Annualized Rate of Occurrence (How often it happens per year).
20. What is ALE? Annualized Loss Expectancy ($SLE \times ARO$).
21. If Likelihood is 5 and Impact is 4 (1-5 scale), what is the score? 20 (High/Critical depending on the matrix).
22. What is a "Threat Vector"? The path or means used to gain access to a system.
23. Define "Exploit." Code or technique used to take advantage of a vulnerability.
24. What is "Countermeasure"? Another term for a security control or mitigation.
25. What is "Gap Analysis"? Comparing current security posture against a desired standard.

Part 2: Global Frameworks - NIST & ISO (26-55)
Objective: Testing adherence to international standards.
26. What is the purpose of NIST SP 800-30? To provide guidance for conducting risk assessments.
27. Name the 6 functions of NIST CSF 2.0. Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover.
28. What is ISO/IEC 27001? An international standard for an Information Security Management System (ISMS).
29. What is ISO/IEC 27005? Guidelines for information security risk management.
30. What is the "Identify" function in NIST CSF? Developing an understanding to manage risk to systems and data.
31. What is "Protect" in NIST CSF? Developing safeguards to ensure delivery of services.
32. What is "Detect" in NIST CSF? Identifying the occurrence of a cybersecurity event.
33. What is "Respond" in NIST CSF? Taking action regarding a detected cybersecurity incident.
34. What is "Recover" in NIST CSF? Restoring capabilities impaired due to a cyber incident.
35. What is "Govern" in NIST CSF 2.0? Establishing the organization’s cybersecurity risk strategy.
36. What is SOC2 Type II? A report on the effectiveness of controls over a period of time.
37. What is the CIA Triad? Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.
38. Define "Confidentiality." Ensuring data is accessible only to authorized users.
39. Define "Integrity." Ensuring data is accurate and has not been tampered with.
40. Define "Availability." Ensuring systems and data are accessible when needed.
41. What is "Defense in Depth"? A layered security approach using multiple controls.
42. What is the Principle of Least Privilege? Granting users only the access they need for their job.
43. What is a "Security Control"? A safeguard to avoid, detect, or minimize security risks.
44. What is a "Technical Control"? Security implemented via hardware or software (e.g., Firewall).
45. What is an "Administrative Control"? Security via policies or procedures (e.g., training).
46. What is a "Physical Control"? Security for physical assets (e.g., locks, cameras).
47. What is a "Detective Control"? Controls that find an incident after it starts (e.g., IDS).
48. What is a "Preventive Control"? Controls that stop an incident before it starts (e.g., MFA).
49. What is a "Corrective Control"? Controls that fix systems after an incident (e.g., backups).
50. What is "Asset Inventory"? A comprehensive list of all IT assets in the organization.
51-55. Check NIST Tiers: Tier 1 (Partial) to Tier 4 (Adaptive).

Part 3: Indian Compliance - DPDP & CERT-In (56-90)
Objective: Testing localized Indian regulatory knowledge.
56. What is the DPDP Act 2023? Digital Personal Data Protection Act of India.
57. Who is a "Data Fiduciary"? The entity determining the purpose of data processing.
58. Who is a "Data Principal"? The individual whose data is being processed.
59. Who is a "Data Processor"? An entity processing data on behalf of a Fiduciary.
60. What is "Consent" under DPDP? Must be free, specific, informed, unconditional, and clear.
61. Can consent be withdrawn under DPDP? Yes, at any time by the Data Principal.
62. What is a "Significant Data Fiduciary" (SDF)? A fiduciary designated by Govt based on volume/sensitivity.
63. Is a DPO mandatory for an SDF? Yes, they must appoint a Data Protection Officer.
64. What is the penalty for a data breach under DPDP? Up to ₹250 crore.
65. What is the penalty for failing to notify a breach? Up to ₹200 crore.
66. What is CERT-In? Indian Computer Emergency Response Team.
67. What is the reporting window for cyber incidents? Within 6 hours of detection.
68. How long must ICT logs be stored in India? 180 days (within Indian jurisdiction).
69. What is the "Right to Erasure" in DPDP? The right to have personal data deleted.
70. What is "Notice" under DPDP? Information provided to the user before seeking consent.
71. Do Indian laws apply to data processed outside India? Yes, if it involves offering goods/services to Indians.
72. What is a "Personal Data Breach"? Unauthorized processing or accidental disclosure of data.
73. What is the RBI's role in cyber risk? Sets security guidelines for banks and NBFCs in India.
74. What is SEBI’s role in cyber risk? Sets cybersecurity frameworks for stock brokers and exchanges.
75. What is the "6-hour rule" source? CERT-In directions issued in April 2022.
76. Can children’s data be processed for tracking? No, DPDP prohibits tracking or targeted ads for children.
77. Who is a "Consent Manager"? A platform to manage/withdraw consent for users.
78. What is "Data Localization" in India? Requirement to store certain data within Indian borders.
79. What is a "Sudarshan" or similar audit? Reference to CERT-In empaneled security audits.
80. What is "Right to Grievance Redressal"? User's right to complain about data misuse.
81-90. Scenario questions: e.g., "If I lose 1000 records, do I notify?" (Yes).

92. What is "Phishing"? Deceptive emails used to steal credentials or data.
93. What is "Spear Phishing"? Targeted phishing aimed at a specific person or org.
94. What is "Whaling"? Phishing targeted at high-level executives (CEOs).
95. What is a "Zero-Day"? An exploit for a vulnerability unknown to the vendor.
96. What is a "DDoS" attack? Flooding a system with traffic to make it unavailable.
97. What is "SQL Injection"? Inserting malicious code into a database query.
98. What is "Cross-Site Scripting" (XSS)? Injecting malicious scripts into trusted websites.

101. What is "Business Email Compromise" (BEC)? Scammers impersonating executives to redirect funds.
102. What is "Man-in-the-Middle" (MitM)? An attacker intercepting communication between two parties.
103. What is "Brute Force"? Attempting all possible passwords until one works.
104. What is "Credential Stuffing"? Using stolen passwords from one site to login to others.
105. What is "Social Engineering"? Psychological manipulation to gain access or info.
106. What is "Vishing"? Phishing via voice calls or VoIP.
107. What is "Smishing"? Phishing via SMS text messages.
108. What is a "Rootkit"? Malware designed to provide hidden administrative access.
109. What is "Trojan"? Malicious software disguised as legitimate software.
110. What is "Spyware"? Software that secretly monitors user activity.
111. What is an "Insider Threat"? Risk from employees or contractors within the org.
112. What is "Data Exfiltration"? Unauthorized transfer of data from a computer.
113. What is a "Supply Chain Attack"? Attacking a vendor to reach their customers (e.g., SolarWinds).
114. What is "Juice Jacking"? Hacking a device via a public USB charging port.
115. What is "Shoulder Surfing"? Looking over someone's shoulder to see their screen/PIN.

124. What is an "EDR"? Endpoint Detection and Response.
